Health Journaling Mobile App

Competitive Analysis Personas User Interviews Usability Testing Wireflows UI Design Prototypes

Objective

  • Test a hypothesis that a health journaling app would empower people to record and analyze their symptoms, treatments, and passive data to better understand their health
  • Use Lean UX processes to gather early feedback and iterate for a more complete and polished product

Role

  • I led UX Strategy, research, and design working with the product owner and two native developers

Process

  • We conducted market research to find a gap in the market for this need
  • I conducted discovery research through interviews with physicians and chronic condition sufferers
  • I created a clickable prototype to continue research
  • We developed MVPs and continued to gather feedback and iterate

Complexities

  • Designing for users with sensitive health data
  • Creating a flow to capture daily entries of everything they need to record with as little effort as possible
  • Creating reports and visualizations that users and physicians can find valuable

Results

  • Two native apps with five star rating and thousands of users
  • The flexibility to record whatever symptoms, treatments, and notes a user would like as well as general health, passive wearable data, and weather data

Hardware-as-a-Service Startup Web App

  • The system also needed to create rules for controlling sockets to save energy

  • I worked with the Head of Design along with two developers and the client's internal team
  • I took the design lead after the research phase to establish the information architecture and design the user interface

  • I helped analyze and synthesize the research using an affinity diagram and opportunity solution trees
  • I then worked closely with the client to create annotated wireflows in Miro followed by a high fidelity interactive prototype in Figma to convey the vision to the client and developers

Exercise Booking App

User Research Paper Prototyping Wireflows Pricing Model UI Patterns / Style Guides Product Requirements

Objective

  • Create a flexible exercise booking app (studio classes, trainer sessions, and gym day passes)
  • It needed an admin backend to manage inventory and users

Role

  • I worked with a product owner and a graphic designer to bring this product to life
  • I created the algorithm for the dynamic pricing model

Process

  • We created personas based on market research and then decided what needs the MVP had to fill
  • We designed the UX for the app and admin portal by using a combination of whiteboarding, paper prototyping, and creating wireflows
  • We made a prototype to test, iterate based on feedback, and to better convey the experience to the development team
  • We interviewed multiple development agencies, then managed the project and development team until the release of the app

Complexities

  • Creating a product that would be attractive to employers, insurers, end-users, and fitness facilitators
  • The admin web app was the most complicated aspect because of the different admin roles and the control needed

Results

  • Two native mobile apps and a web app administration tool with three different admin types and permissions
  • In 2019 the largest health insurer in the Philadelphia area became a client and offered this product to its population
