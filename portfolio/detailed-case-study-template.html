01 / Context

The product, team, and moment.

Describe the organization, product, audience, market moment, team structure, and why this work mattered. Include the relevant background someone would need to understand the decisions that followed.

Useful details

  • Business model or domain
  • Audience and user types
  • Team size and disciplines
  • Timeline and constraints

02 / Challenge

What needed to be solved.

"Frame the central tension here: the product risk, user pain, business pressure, technical constraint, or alignment problem that made the work difficult."

User risk

What users struggled with, avoided, misunderstood, or needed.

Business risk

What adoption, revenue, cost, delivery, or retention issue mattered.

Delivery risk

What made the product hard to build, prioritize, align, or ship.

03 / Discovery

How the team found clarity.

Research Inputs

  • Stakeholder interviews and business goals
  • User interviews, observation, or usability testing
  • Analytics, support data, workflows, and product audit
  • Competitive or market signals

Questions Answered

  • What problem are we really solving?
  • Which assumptions are riskiest?
  • What should be validated before building?
  • What does success need to look like?

04 / Strategy

The product direction.

Explain the strategic choice: what the team decided to focus on, what it chose not to do, and why that direction created the best path toward user value and business value.

Principles

  • Decision principle or product bet
  • Design principle or experience goal
  • Delivery principle or sequencing rule

Roadmap

  • Phase 1: learning or MVP
  • Phase 2: adoption or workflow depth
  • Phase 3: scale, automation, or expansion

05 / Execution

How the work moved from idea to shipped product.

Prototype

Make the direction tangible.

Show how concepts, flows, journey maps, prototypes, or technical spikes helped the team evaluate direction before investing heavily.

Validate

Test the riskiest assumptions.

Describe feedback loops, usability tests, stakeholder reviews, analytics, pilot groups, or experiments that changed the direction.

Ship

Align the team around delivery.

Explain how product, design, engineering, and stakeholders stayed aligned through prioritization, scope decisions, tradeoffs, and launch.

Artifacts

Evidence and deliverables.

Screenshot, journey map, or workflow artifact
Prototype, roadmap, research output, or UI sample

06 / Outcomes

What changed.

Metric

Adoption, conversion, retention, time saved, revenue, or delivery speed.

Metric

A second measurable outcome or strong qualitative result.

Metric

Team, process, business, or user impact worth highlighting.

Summarize the final impact in plain language. Explain what became possible after the work and connect the result back to the original challenge.

07 / Reflection

What this demonstrates.

Use this section to connect the work back to your leadership strengths: clarity, decision-making, facilitation, design judgment, research quality, delivery partnership, AI-enabled workflows, or product strategy.

Key takeaway

A concise statement that explains why this case study matters and what a hiring manager or client should remember.
